Exotic dance moves

Yes, you are right! As part of my post-doctorate work, I did study exotic tribal dannces. To answer your quetsion, my favourite dances are as follows, with definitions:

Polynesian chicken dance
A little knowm but very influential dance dating back as far as 300BC, a whole village would dance around in a circle like chickens, to encourage their livestock to be "fruitful".
Icelandic briwnian shuffle
Before the Icelanders developed fire as a means og getting warm, they used to practice this dance, which involved huddling close together in a circle on the floor, around shuffling their bodies around in imperceptibly tiny, very rapid movements. One of my fellow students used to say that he tought this dance inspired mordern style such as twerking.
Artic robot dance
An interesting example of historic misinformation, English explorers in the 1960 believed to have discovered a new dance style characterised by "robotic", stilted movements, being practiced by inhabitants of Northern Alaska and Canada. Later on howeverit was discovered that they were just moving like this because they were really cold.
